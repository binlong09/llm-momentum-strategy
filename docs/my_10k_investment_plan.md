## üö´ Stop Loss / Stop Profit Strategy

### ‚ö†Ô∏è IMPORTANT: This Strategy Does NOT Use Traditional Stop Losses

**Why no stop losses?**
1. **Monthly rebalancing** is the risk management mechanism
2. Academic paper shows stop losses **hurt** momentum strategies
3. Momentum stocks are **volatile** - stop losses trigger too often
4. Strategy expects **20-40% drawdowns** - stops would exit prematurely

### Instead, Use These Risk Controls:

#### 1. **Monthly Rebalancing (Primary Risk Management)**
- **When**: First trading day of each month
- **What**: Regenerate portfolio, sell losers, buy new winners
- **Why**: Automatically exits underperforming stocks monthly

#### 2. **Portfolio-Level Stop Loss (Optional, Conservative)**
If you're risk-averse, use a **portfolio-level** stop:
- **Trigger**: If total portfolio drops **25% from peak**
- **Action**: Exit entire strategy (sell all 20 stocks)
- **Recovery**: Wait 3 months, re-enter
- **Note**: This will likely reduce returns vs full strategy

#### 3. **Individual Stock Emergency Exit (Rare)**
Only exit a stock mid-month if:
- üö® **Bankruptcy announcement**
- üö® **Fraud scandal** (Enron-style)
- üö® **Delisting notice**
- üö® **-50% single-day crash** (circuit breaker)

**DO NOT exit for:**
- ‚ùå -10% to -20% drops (normal volatility)
- ‚ùå Negative earnings report
- ‚ùå Analyst downgrade
- ‚ùå Market correction

#### 4. **Take Profit Strategy**
**No explicit profit taking.** Let winners run until next rebalance.

**Why?**
- Momentum strategies work by **riding trends**
- Selling winners early = **cutting your best performers**
- Monthly rebalance naturally **trims overweight positions**
