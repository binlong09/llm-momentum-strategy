## Alert Severity Levels

### üö® CRITICAL (Immediate Attention Required)

**What it means:**
- Stock dropped >15% in one day
- Red flag news detected (fraud, bankruptcy, scandal)
- Extreme concentration risk (>30% of portfolio)

**Default action:** Review immediately, likely sell

**Frequency:** Rare (0-2 per month in healthy portfolio)

---

### ‚ö†Ô∏è WARNING (Monitor Closely)

**What it means:**
- Stock dropped 10-15% in one day
- Negative news (downgrades, earnings miss, layoffs)
- High concentration (20-30% of portfolio)
- Multiple negative signals accumulating

**Default action:** Research and monitor, usually hold until monthly rebalance

**Frequency:** Occasional (2-5 per month)

---

### ‚ÑπÔ∏è INFO (For Awareness)

**What it means:**
- Stock moved 5-10% (up or down)
- Positive news (upgrades, earnings beat)
- Normal market volatility
- Take-profit opportunities (>20% gains)

**Default action:** Note and continue, no action needed

**Frequency:** Common (5-15 per month)

## When to Act vs When to Hold

### üî¥ ACT IMMEDIATELY (Sell Today)

**Only in these scenarios:**

1. **Fraud/Scandal**
   - Company accused of accounting fraud
   - CEO/CFO arrested or indicted
   - SEC investigation announced
   - **Example:** Wirecard, Enron-style collapse
   - **Action:** SELL ALL shares immediately

2. **Bankruptcy Risk**
   - Company announces liquidity crisis
   - Bond yields spike dramatically
   - Credit downgrade to junk status
   - Missing debt payments
   - **Example:** SVB Bank collapse scenario
   - **Action:** SELL ALL shares immediately

3. **Catastrophic Event**
   - Stock down >20% on fundamental deterioration
   - Major product recall affecting revenue
   - Lost major customer (>50% of revenue)
   - Regulatory ban on core product
   - **Example:** Boeing 737 MAX grounding
   - **Action:** SELL ALL shares, reassess later

4. **Momentum Completely Broken**
   - Stock ranked in top 5, now dropped to #100+
   - Persistent underperformance for 2+ weeks
   - Multiple negative catalysts stacking
   - **Example:** Stock was #3, now #87 after bad news
   - **Action:** SELL, replace with better momentum

---

### üü° MONITOR CLOSELY (Research, Usually Hold)

**In these scenarios:**

1. **Earnings Miss**
   - Missed by <10%, guidance maintained
   - Stock down 10-15%
   - Company still profitable and growing
   - **Example:** "We grew 20% instead of expected 25%"
   - **Action:** READ earnings call transcript ‚Üí Usually HOLD
   - **Why:** One miss doesn't break momentum

2. **Analyst Downgrade**
   - Single analyst downgrade (not consensus)
   - No fundamental change
   - Stock down 5-12%
   - **Example:** "Morgan Stanley downgrades from Buy to Hold"
   - **Action:** Check if other analysts agree ‚Üí Usually HOLD
   - **Why:** Analyst opinions are often noise

3. **CEO/Executive Departure**
   - Planned succession announced
   - Retirement or "spend time with family"
   - Stock down <15%
   - **Example:** CEO stepping down after 10 years
   - **Action:** Research reason ‚Üí Usually HOLD
   - **Why:** Management changes happen, don't panic

4. **Sector Rotation**
   - Whole sector down together
   - No company-specific news
   - Market-wide sell-off
   - **Example:** All tech stocks down 5-8%
   - **Action:** Check if sector still has momentum ‚Üí HOLD
   - **Why:** Temporary sector weakness is normal

5. **Guidance Cut**
   - Lowered but still positive
   - External factors (economy, rates)
   - Stock down 10-15%
   - **Example:** "Growth forecast 15% vs prior 18%"
   - **Action:** Assess if temporary ‚Üí Usually HOLD
   - **Why:** Markets overreact to guidance changes

---

### üü¢ NO ACTION NEEDED (Business as Usual)

**In these scenarios:**

1. **Normal Volatility**
   - Stock up/down <10% without news
   - Entire market volatile
   - Intraday swings
   - **Action:** IGNORE completely
   - **Why:** Normal market behavior

2. **Positive News**
   - Earnings beat
   - Analyst upgrades
   - Partnership announcements
   - Stock up 5-15%
   - **Action:** Feel good, HOLD until monthly rebalance
   - **Why:** Let winners run

3. **Large Gains**
   - Stock up 20-30% since purchase
   - Still in top 25 portfolio
   - Momentum still strong
   - **Action:** HOLD, take profits at monthly rebalance
   - **Why:** Momentum can continue for months

4. **Small Moves**
   - Stock up/down 2-5%
   - No significant news
   - **Action:** IGNORE
   - **Why:** Market noise

| Weight | Severity | Action |
|--------|----------|--------|

## Action Checklists

### üö® Critical Alert Checklist

When you see a CRITICAL alert:

- [ ] **Read the news** - Understand what happened
- [ ] **Verify the source** - Is it credible?
- [ ] **Check the facts** - Fraud? Bankruptcy? Or overreaction?
- [ ] **Assess damage** - Temporary or permanent?
- [ ] **Decision:**
  - [ ] SELL if: Fraud, bankruptcy, or fundamental break
  - [ ] HOLD if: Overreaction to fixable problem
- [ ] **Execute immediately** - Don't wait
- [ ] **Document** - Note why you sold for future learning

**Time to decision: <1 hour**

---

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Warning Alert Checklist

When you see a WARNING alert:

- [ ] **Read the details** - What triggered this?
- [ ] **Research context** - Is it company-specific or market-wide?
- [ ] **Check momentum rank** - Did it change significantly?
- [ ] **Review similar stocks** - Are peers affected too?
- [ ] **Assess trend** - Is this an isolated event or pattern?
- [ ] **Decision:**
  - [ ] SELL if: Multiple warnings + momentum broken
  - [ ] HOLD if: Isolated event + momentum intact
- [ ] **Set reminder** - Check again in 3-5 days
- [ ] **Note for rebalance** - May need to sell at month-end

**Time to decision: Same day**

---

### ‚ÑπÔ∏è Info Alert Checklist

When you see an INFO alert:

- [ ] **Quick scan** - What's the headline?
- [ ] **Note it** - Mental bookmark
- [ ] **Decision:**
  - [ ] Almost always HOLD
- [ ] **Move on** - Don't overthink it

**Time to decision: 30 seconds**

## Common Mistakes

### ‚ùå Mistake #1: Panic Selling on Normal Volatility

**Scenario:**
```
Stock down 8% on no news
You panic and sell
Next day: Stock bounces back 10%
```

**Why it's wrong:**
- 5-10% moves are NORMAL
- Momentum stocks are volatile
- Selling = locking in losses
- You bought for momentum, not stability

**Correct approach:**
- Only sell on >15% drops with bad news
- Trust the monthly rebalance process
- Volatility ‚â† broken momentum

---

### ‚ùå Mistake #2: Ignoring Red Flags

**Scenario:**
```
Alert: Company under fraud investigation
You think: "It'll blow over, I'll hold"
Result: Stock down 60%, investigation proven
```

**Why it's wrong:**
- Red flags rarely "blow over"
- Hope is not a strategy
- Momentum doesn't survive scandals

**Correct approach:**
- Take red flag alerts seriously
- When in doubt, SELL and move on
- Better safe than sorry

---

### ‚ùå Mistake #3: Overreacting to Analyst Noise

**Scenario:**
```
Single analyst downgrades stock
You panic and sell immediately
Stock continues momentum, up 25% next month
```

**Why it's wrong:**
- Analysts are often wrong
- One opinion ‚â† trend
- You sold a winner on noise

**Correct approach:**
- Check if it's consensus or outlier
- Look at data, not opinions
- Hold unless fundamentals changed

---

### ‚ùå Mistake #4: Cutting Winners Too Early

**Scenario:**
```
Stock up 20%, you take profits
Stock continues to 80%+ gain
You left 60% on the table
```

**Why it's wrong:**
- Momentum stocks can run for months
- Cutting winners = capping your upside
- Best performers need time

**Correct approach:**
- Let winners run until monthly rebalance
- Only trim if concentration risk (>20%)
- Momentum = riding trends, not timing tops

---

### ‚ùå Mistake #5: Death by 1000 Cuts

**Scenario:**
```
You sell stocks on every 10% dip
End up with cash as market rallies
Miss gains while "waiting for entry"
```

**Why it's wrong:**
- Market timing doesn't work
- Momentum persists through dips
- You can't predict bottoms

**Correct approach:**
- Stick to monthly rebalance schedule
- Only sell on critical alerts
- Accept volatility as part of the game
